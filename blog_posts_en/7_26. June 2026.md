# MATIK Competition 2026

On May 10, the Tabasalu kart track hosted another VHK MATIK course electric formula car competition, in which three teams took part with cars they had built themselves: Beegreen, Vurts, and Estimators.
The goal of the hour-long competition was to drive the electric formula car as far as possible.
Building the cars for the competition, with the support of the instructors, took a whole school year's worth of effort and dedication. The weekly MATIK class allowed for communication, cooperation, and discussion about the electric formula cars' materials, electronics, sponsors, construction, etc.
For the competition, we checked over all the tools and cars to make sure they would definitely come along.
Before the start, VHK engineering program alumni inspected the cars, using the actual Greenpower competition regulations as the benchmark.
After that came a warm-up run, during which the teams could see whether their cars were ready to race.
In addition to the teams and alumni, teachers and parents also came along to cheer them on.
Competition on the track was fierce, with constant passing and maneuvering. Right from the start, team Vurts rose to first place and continued to lead until the end of the competition. Second place went to team Beegreen, who narrowly lost out to Vurts on speed. Third place went to Estimators, whose car drove steadily and fought to the very last effort. The prize for first and second place was financial support from the school, which the teams can use to cover the costs of a trip to a competition abroad.
All teams gained driving experience and found something to improve on their car.
MATIK course instructor Javier, who has been working on projects like this for four years already, said that everyone did very well in the competition and that he is very proud of the teams' effort, work input, and ingenuity.
The event itself was well organized and planned: the schedule allowed room to breathe, all the necessary tools were on hand, and the competition itself ran smoothly.
Luckily the weather outside was pleasant, everyone was in good spirits, and the competition ended on a positive note.
