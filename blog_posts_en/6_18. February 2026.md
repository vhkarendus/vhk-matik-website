# VHK Engineering Day 2026

On February 12 took place the VHK Engineering Day 2026, bringing together representatives of education, business, and the public sector to discuss the role and opportunities of engineering education.
The meaning of engineering in society and business was addressed, and ideas were exchanged on how to support young people's lasting interest in and study of engineering. Representatives of the state, professional associations, and companies alike shared their views: Helle Hallik, Chief Expert in Mathematics at the Ministry of Education, and Liina Kanter, Head of the Education Innovation Department; Martti Kiisa, Rector of Tallinn Technical University of Applied Sciences; Enn Kerner, Vice President of the Estonian Association of Engineers; Veljo Konnimois, founder of Radius Machining; and others.
Participating in the discussion were Kristi Kuusemets, Development Manager of the HARNO Engineering Academy; Evelin Teiva, Director of Kadrina Secondary School; Reivo Maasik, physics teacher at Tallinn Secondary Science School, and Kaisa Tamkivi, Development Manager; Ivo Visak, Head of "TI Hüpe" and teacher of future technologies at Saaremaa State Upper Secondary School; and others.
Also significant was the contribution of VHK upper secondary students, who presented engineering education at the school and took part in the discussions.
We wish to strengthen cooperation between the fields of education and business and to shape joint steps in developing engineering education. The day ended with a lively exchange of ideas over coffee.
VHK thanks all speakers and participants for their meaningful contribution!
